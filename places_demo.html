Different Places of articulation

manner held: plosive vowel held: /a/ ▽ varying: place · lips → velum

Hold the vowel still (the downward triangle for /a/) and the manner still (a plosive — a straight line). Now only one thing moves: as the sound travels from the lips back to the soft palate, the line slides from the apex of the triangle toward its base.
